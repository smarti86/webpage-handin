Handin Webpage

Hello! I'm Sandra and I initially set out to my the website of my favorite consignment shop, The Elite Repeat, more accessible. I've been shopping at The Elite Repeat for a decade with my mom, and while at home for the long weekend this semester, we decided to visit once more. However, with their popularity comes crazy hours. "Why don't you check the website and see the hours there" my mom suggested after a failed attempt at calling the store to see if it was open. When I did check The Elite Repeat website, I was horrified by how outdatted and hard it was to find the hours information. With this assignment around the corner I decided to pick on the website and do something about the bad design and low visual contrast.

Figma Mockup vs Implementation

When working on the header + navigation bar for my website, I deviated from my Figma mockup by adding a navigation bar to go to the "about" "directions" "hours" and "contact us" section faster. This increased accessiblity and eased navigation because the user can now look at the navigation bar, click on the information they are looking for, and they are be automatically scrolled down to that part of the webpage.



Because I added the navigation bar in my implemention, I decided to inverte the light blue background for the dark blue background, as seen above. This helped increase the visual contrast. The header text is more legible in white than it was in black.

I also changed my font from JejuMyeongjo to Arial, sans-serif. While the JejuMyeongjo was pretty, it was too thin and when I added font weight, the letters blended together. To keep it simple and to maximize visual contrast, I decided to change the font to Arial, sans-serif. Similarly, I abandoned the orange hues in the color scheme of my visual style guide because the WaveAIM report marked the orange hues as low contrast. When I created my color scheme, I wasn't considering visual contrast as much as I was aesthetics. To find a new pop of color, I looked a the central image of the three stylish women around the couch and pulled the maroon color from there.

The rest of my webpage implementation was close to what I designed in my Figma mockup. I did change the layout of pages and added shadows to increase visual contrast and ultimately make the page more legible.
